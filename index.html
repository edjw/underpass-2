Underpass 2

A sampling companion for Elektron Model:Samples™ devices

Input Level

What is this?

Underpass 2 turns your Model:Samples into a full sampler. Record from any audio source on your computer and transfer samples to your device over USB — no Elektron Transfer app needed.

Requirements

  • Model:Samples connected via USB
  • Chrome, Edge, Brave, or Firefox (v108+). Safari/iOS not supported (no WebMIDI)

How to use

  1. Connect your Model:Samples via USB and power it on
  2. Click “Enable Audio” and grant microphone/MIDI permissions
  3. Select your Audio In — choose any line input to sample external audio, or “Model:Samples” to resample your device’s output
  4. Select MIDI In and MIDI Out — these should auto-select if your device is connected
  5. Set a Sample ID (0–16383) and optionally a Sample Name
  6. Watch the Input Level meter to confirm signal
  7. Hit Record (max 10 seconds) then Stop
  8. The sample transfers automatically via MIDI — watch the progress bar

Finding your samples on the device

Transferred samples land in the INCOMING folder on the +Drive. To use one:

  1. Press [WAVE] to open the Sample Assign menu
  2. Navigate to the INCOMING folder
  3. Select your sample and press LEVEL/DATA to assign it to the active track

Audio setup options

There are two ways to get audio into Underpass 2:

USB audio (simplest) — The Model:Samples streams audio over USB. Select “Model:Samples” as your Audio In. No extra hardware or cables needed.

Audio interface — Route the device’s Main Out or Headphones into your audio interface’s inputs. Select your interface as the Audio In. This is useful if you want to sample external sources alongside the device, or if your setup already runs through an interface.

Resampling your device

To capture what’s playing on your device as a new sample, select “Model:Samples” (USB) or your audio interface (if the device’s outputs are connected to it) as the Audio In. Whatever the device is playing — patterns, live pad hits, effects — gets recorded and sent straight back as a new sample. Great for layering sounds, capturing jams, or creating one-shot variations.

Tips

  • Sample ID auto-increments after each successful transfer (resets to 0 on page reload)
  • Audio is recorded at 48 kHz, 16-bit, summed to mono — matching the device’s native format
  • Recordings are capped at 10 seconds
  • If transfer stalls, check your USB connection and ensure MIDI ports are correctly selected
